Day 02 · 7:00 PM · Jean Dry Lake Bed, Mojave Desert

Rise Lantern Festival

The desert goes quiet. Thousands of people hold their lanterns. At once — they release. Warm light lifts from every direction, rising past you, above you, until the entire sky is glowing. You’ll write a message on yours. Then let it go.

What You Need to Know

🔥
The lantern release happens at exactly 7 PM. Be at the Compass Stage by 6:30. Position yourself center-crowd — the best view is lanterns rising on all sides of you.
🌡️
Desert temperature drops 25°F after sunset. Bring a hoodie and a blanket. It’s warm during the festival, then cold fast once the sun’s gone.
🚗
No Uber or Lyft. We’ll drive ourselves — 30 min from the Strip. Parking is $30, pre-purchased with tickets. Expect traffic leaving after.
📶
Almost no cell service on the lake bed. Screenshot tickets and download playlists before we leave. That’s part of the magic.
✏️
Write your message before the ceremony. Everyone writes something on their lantern — a wish, a tribute, a goal. Take your time with it. Markers are provided.

Brady’s Note

The Narrows is one of the most unique hikes on Earth. You’re literally walking through a river inside a canyon. Bring a dry bag for your phone. The water is cold but you won’t care — the walls towering above you are something else entirely.

The Details

Getting There

Fly into LAS (Harry Reid). Rent a car — you need it for Zion and the festival. ~$40/day.

Rise Tickets

Gold tier ($269) — skip the lines, VIP restrooms. Worth the upgrade over Silver.

Zion Shuttle

Free. Runs every 5-10 min from Visitor Center. First shuttle 7 AM. No reservation needed.

Gear Rental

Narrows shoes + stick from Zion Outfitter in Springdale. ~$35/person. Reserve ahead.

Time Zones

Vegas is Pacific. Zion is Mountain. You lose 1 hour driving east. Plan accordingly.

Best Window

Early October. Rise Festival dates. Zion is 65-75°F, fall colors, trails open, crowds thin.
